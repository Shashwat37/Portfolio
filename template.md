# Design Prompt: "Sawad" Portfolio Template

A detailed design brief for recreating this template's look and feel — useful for handing to a designer or feeding into an AI website/image generation tool.

## Overall Concept
A minimal, dark-mode-first personal portfolio for a software engineer/creative professional. The tone is confident and editorial — big serif/sans display type, generous whitespace, and a "digital resume meets creative studio" feel. Built as a multi-page site (not a single long-scroll), with dedicated pages for Projects, Experience, Tools, and Thoughts (blog).

## Layout & Structure

- **Sticky top navigation**: logo/name on the left, horizontal text links (Home, Projects, Experience, Tools, Thoughts) center or right, with a subtle CTA button.
- **Hero section**: full-bleed portrait photo (tall, editorial-style headshot) paired with name, title ("Software Engineer"), and a one-line tagline. Social icons (Dribbble, Twitter/X, Instagram, email) sit near the photo.
- **Stats row**: three-column counter block — "+12 Years of Experience," "+46 Projects Completed," "+20 Worldwide Clients" — large numbers with small uppercase labels underneath.
- **Skills/marquee strip**: horizontally scrolling or static text bands like "DYNAMIC ANIMATION, MOTION DESIGN" and "FRAMER, FIGMA, WORDPRESS, REACTJS," each linking to a relevant page — reads like a tag cloud/ticker.
- **Recent Projects grid**: 2–3 column card grid, each card an image thumbnail with title overlay, linking out to case studies.
- **Experience timeline**: stacked list of roles (company name, role description, date range) — text-heavy, no icons, relying on typographic hierarchy.
- **Tools/stack section**: small grid of tool logos + one-line descriptions (Framer, Figma, Lemon Squeezy, ChatGPT, Notion, Next.js).
- **Blog/Thoughts preview**: 2–3 article cards with title, short excerpt, date, and read-time.
- **Contact section ("Let's Work Together")**: simple form — Name, Email, Budget (dropdown: <$3k, $3k–$5k, $5k–$10k, >$10k), Message, Submit button.
- **Footer**: minimal credit line ("Made by ___ | Powered by Framer").

## Typography

- Large, bold display headline for section titles (e.g., "SOFTWARE ENGINEER," "RECENT PROJECTS") in all-caps, tight tracking.
- Clean sans-serif body copy for descriptions — high readability, medium contrast against background.
- Strong size contrast between hero name/title and supporting text creates the editorial hierarchy.

## Color & Visual Style

- Likely a dark or muted neutral background (black/charcoal or off-white) with high-contrast text — typical of premium Framer portfolio templates in this genre.
- Photography-driven hero (professional portrait) rather than illustration or 3D render.
- Flat, borderless cards; dividers via whitespace and thin hairlines rather than heavy borders/shadows.

## Interaction & Motion

- Scroll-triggered fade/slide-in animations for sections (standard Framer pattern).
- Hover states on project cards (subtle scale or image zoom) and nav links (underline or color shift).
- Possible marquee/ticker animation on the skills tags row.
- Smooth page transitions between Home/Projects/Experience/Tools/Thoughts since it's multi-page.

## Tone & Use Case

Built for individual professionals (developers, designers) who want a personal-brand site that reads as polished and hire-able — a resume, portfolio, and blog combined, with a lead-gen contact form scoped by project budget.
